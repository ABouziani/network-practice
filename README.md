## üìã Instructions

### Phase 1: Profile Analysis & Research
* **Audit** your current LinkedIn and GitHub profiles - identify areas for improvement.
* **Research** 2-3 strong examples of LinkedIn and GitHub profiles in your field.
* **Analyze inspiring professionals** - note best practices and engagement strategies.
* **Research active open source projects** in your technology stack or areas of interest.

### Phase 2: LinkedIn Optimization
* **Complete your LinkedIn profile** including:
    * Professional photo (consistent across platforms)
    * Clear headline targeted to your intended role
    * Strong **About summary** showcasing career goals and skills
    * Experience, skills, and education with detailed descriptions
    * External links (portfolio, GitHub)
* **Build at least 100 new connections** from your specialization field and recruiters.
* **Post at least 5 posts** about previously worked curriculum projects or a new skill learned to showcase your learning journey.

### Phase 3: GitHub Enhancement & Open Source Engagement
#### GitHub Profile Organization:
* Organize your GitHub profile to include:
    * Personal profile **README** introducing yourself.
    * At least **two pinned projects** showcasing your best work.
    * Documented **READMEs** with clear usage instructions for all major projects.
    * Clean, well-structured, and commented code.
* **Follow at least 10 people from campus** to build your GitHub network.

#### GitHub Exploration & Community Engagement:
* **Fork at least 5 interesting repositories** relevant to your field or learning goals.
* **Star and watch projects** you find interesting or useful (minimum 15 repositories).
* **Follow active developers** in your technology stack (minimum 15 developers).
* **Join at least 3 developer communities** (Discord, Slack, Reddit, or DEV.to).
* **Introduce yourself** in these communities and engage in at least 3 discussions.
* **Share one post or comment** about something you've learned or found interesting.

### Phase 4: Peer Collaboration
* **Peer review session** - exchange feedback on profiles with cohort members and help each other attend the goals.
* **Endorse and recommend peers' profiles** to build mutual visibility.
* **Support collective branding** - help strengthen your network's professional presence online.
* **Share interesting repositories** and learning resources with your cohort.

### Phase 5: Market Engagement
* **Demonstrate professional market engagement** through:
    * Recruiter interactions or messages.
    * Connections with HR professionals or industry experts.
    * Participation in professional groups or discussions.
    * Evidence of community engagement and learning from other developers.
    * Sharing knowledge or interesting findings with your network.

## üõ†Ô∏è Tips for Success

* **Consistency is Key:** Use the **same profile photo** across platforms (LinkedIn, GitHub, etc.) for brand recognition.
* **Quality Documentation:** Keep project documentation (READMEs) clean, concise, and understandable.
* **Be Active:** Join LinkedIn discussions, publish thoughtful articles, or comment on industry news.
* **Post-Event Follow-up:** If you attend an event, connect with new contacts on LinkedIn within **24 hours**.
* **Showcase Your Best:** Pin your best GitHub projects and maintain a clean, well-commented codebase.
* **Community Engagement:** Start with simple community participation - introduce yourself and ask questions before attempting major code contributions.
* **Be Genuine:** Focus on learning and sharing rather than outright self-promotion in community interactions.
